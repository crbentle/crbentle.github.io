HISTORY

Every two minutes a woman dies from a pregnancy-related complication, yet over 98% of these deaths are preventable.

Delivering reproductive and child services amid limited resource circumstances can be difficult. When access to care is a challenge due to geography, finances or gender issues, it is imperative to prioritize health interventions by focusing on what evidence supports as realistic and sustainable. Mama Maisha was created in response to this need. Â We work at the village level, where women are often unable to obtain even the most basic prenatal care, either because it is unavailable or because they do not have a way to get to the services.

Recent estimates by the World Health Organization place Tanzania in with other countries that have the highest number of maternal deaths worldwide, and these numbers are unlikely to change in the near future. Mama Maisha is on the ground, empowering women with education and support during pregnancy, postpartum and through the reproductive years. We are a demand-side intervention to decrease the number of women that die from pregnancy related complications, the overwhelming number of which are completely preventable.
